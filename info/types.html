Biophilic Homes

Biophillic homes are one type of biohome.
Some types of biophillic houses include natural light houses, living wall houses, and green roof houses.
Biophillic homes are a product of biophillic design, which is the process of incorporating natural elements into the build.
It tries to focus on the sensory elements of humans, such as visual, auditory, and olfactory senses, by changing things such as color, water, sound, and more.
Biophillic homes aim to reach a design that includes historical, cultural, geographical, spiritual, and/or ecological features in order to strengthen a human’s relationship with their surronding natural environment.
The design is modified to help mimic natural environments that appeal to these senses.
This design helps to connect people to nature

Earthship Homes

These Earthship bio homes are able to use less energy and are more cost-friendly, which is very helpful and nice
They are self sustaining systems, meaning that they can last for a long time; they are durable
They are made from self-recycled or natural material, which is beneficial to the environment
Energy is derived from solar or wind power with small scale windmills and solar panels.
An example of earthship homes is found in the famous Great Wall of China.
Construction methods similar for earthship homes were applied to the construction of the Great Wall Of China.
Construction makes it very resistant to weathering.
Walls range from 19-24 inches which makes it extremely quiet and quickly adapts to changes in noise levels.

Neutral Homes

Neutral homes leaves no carbon footprint behind, which benefits the environment heavily.
They contain solar panels, tight insulation, and water pumps, which are quite bio-friendly.
These home significantly decrease the heating needs required in a regular home.
They are also cost-friendly, which is very good.

These homes are avaliable in any shape and style which can fit the owner's lifestyle.
They are also able to withstand any climate from places such as Arizona to Alaska.
Neutral homes leave the owner of the house with a net zero energy bill.
These neutral homes are high quality, as they are comfortable, healthy, quiet, and sustainable, making these homes very good to live in.

Prefab Houses

A prefab home, or a prefabricated home, is built in a factory and shipped to the location desired
Prefab houses utilize measurements which are exact to ensure that there is no waste left over in the process.
Prefab homes have less waste associated with them, which is beneficial for the environment.
Some prefab homes are constructed with eco-friendly solar panels.
They are constructed in a short amount of time compared to traditional homes. Prefab homes on average are finished in around 5 months, with the earliest being 2 months.

They can also come in various luxury designs.
They are also very cost-effective with a price range of $10,000 to $15,000
Prefab homes have a lower average permit value. The average permit value for a prefab home is $76.80 per square, while a tradiional home has an average permit value of $93.34 per square foot.
